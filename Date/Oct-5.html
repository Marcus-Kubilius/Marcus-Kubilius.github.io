Marcus' Trip to Japan

I only got a few hours of sleep before we left to begin the trip officially. This is our travel day as we have over 24 hours of travelling in front of us before we set foot in our final destination of Seoul, Korea. We arrived a whole 5 minutes before our bus to the airport. Getting to the airport took around an hour then we had a 5-hour flight from Montreal to Calgary, then a 7-hour layover, then a 12-hour plane ride from Calgary to Seoul. It was cool seeing the urbanization of the Island of Montreal change into yellow fields as we went from Quebec to Alberta.

After we arrived in Calgary, we tried to get to a highly-rated Japanese sushi restaurant located somewhere in the airport, and while trying to get to it, we took a wrong turn and ended up going through a no-return door and on the other side of security. Apparently, it is in the American departures terminal which is security-separated from the international/domestic terminals (which are not segregated from each other). At this point, we decided to walk 45 min to a local family-run Filipino restaurant. I got a Pancit and Toki got a Sisig. The sauce for the Pancit was kind of hearty in the same way that MSG is, but a flavour I've never had before. The spices on the chicken were also great. It had a kind of subtle, dry, earthy kick similar to cumin but, again, a flavour, or combination of flavours, that I've never had before. I also got a tarro milkshake.

One benefit of a long flight, we were served alcoholic beverages and hot meals. Speaking of which, the airplane food was perfectly adequate. It certainly wasn't a 5-star dinner but for being 36000 feet in the air in a cramped pressurized cabin which burns thousands of dollars of fuel an hour on a ticket that cost 800 CAD I can't complain. The bread was very dry and the rice was pretty flavourless but the sesame chicken with broccoli and dessert pastry was great. It also came with a spinach, carrot and cherry tomato garden salad which was small but also pretty fresh. We were served a second meal as well which was a Korean glass noodle dish and custard cream cake for dessert.

Since we were travelling west, we chased the sunset for a large portion of the flight which was a nice view. Coincidently, we also caught the end of the sunrise as we were taking off from our first flight to Calgary (see first set of pictures). Originally, the flight was planned to fly over Russia and to the north of Japan however it detoured south over Japan, possibly due to weather formations like the typhoon hitting Taiwan currently.

After finally arriving in Korea, we met up with our friend, Sam, and took the metro for another hour or two. There is a night market literally right around the corner from the hotel we are staying at which gave me deja vu to that Asian festival I went to just before the trip. We walked around for a bit and then decided that we were tired and just grabbed dinner at a convenience store nearby. Unlike in America, convenience stores are not a ripoff (they are actually very affordable) and have great selections of good-tasting ready-to-eat foods.
