Marcus' Trip to Japan

Our hotel in Seoul was very nice. The room is a bit small by Western standards but it is very comfortable. We have a private bathroom with a clean, modern, tall, shower. Apparently, it is widespread for Korean bathrooms to have a drain in the floor between the sink and toilet and the entire bathroom act as the shower so I am relieved that this was not the case for our hotel. Our bathroom is relatively “normal” however the sink is located outside the bathroom (in front of the beds). We have 2 queen beds built as bunk beds essentially. I had my own personal fortress of solitude within the top bunk - or I would if there wasn’t a large mirror on top of the sink that made it so that the top and bottom bunks could look at each other at times. The only gripe we had about the hotel was that the pillows are both very hard and also very puffed out so when laying on them, your neck feels way too elevated. I gave up on using them and have been sleeping without a pillow without any issues. In contrast, our Busan hotel was very not nice. The room itself was honestly fine. The mattresses were on the thinner side but still comfortable, the pillows were much more comfortable than the ones in our Seoul hotel, the climate control was easy to use and there were no hidden cameras which is a legitimate concern in South Korea. On the other hand, I have nothing positive to say about the bathroom. There was a pool of water around the toilet and mold growing on the toilet’s seal. The toilet was missing part of its handle, the toilet paper was one-ply, and there were no towels in the room (you have to go down and request them). We decided to skip showering that night and to avoid using the bathroom as much as possible. To be fair, it was very cheap. When we initially planned the trip, with four of us going to Korea, it worked out that we were all paying less than 8 USD each for the night. We were very happy to return to our hotel in Seoul after that experience. Below is the one photo we took of our hotel in Seoul.

In the morning, we had a traditional pork soup breakfast called Dwaeji Gukbab. This is a local specialty dish regional to Busan. To eat it, you mix the rice and the noodles into the pork soup. Then you the 3 condiments (gochujang paste, salted shrimp sauce, and soy sauce) until you have a broth specialized for you. The salted shrimp sauce was very salty. It literally tasted like shredded shrimp in seawater but in a good way. I cannot emphasize how much it tasted like seawater (but in a good way). It was very filling and only cost 9000 won (just over $9 CAD). One thing I'm really enjoying about buying things in Korea is that prices all have tax included and there is no tipping.

After breakfast, we took a local bus to Haedong Yonggungsa Temple. It is a beautiful temple built right on the coast. I didn't research much into this temple and frankly forgot we were going to it so I don't have much more to say about it other than it had amazing views and was well-maintained.

After the temple, we stopped at a seaside cafe and had a tranquil break in the sea breeze. There was a local cat who slowly edged his way over and lay down on the deck we were enjoying our drinks on. Once our drinks were finished, we took a skytrain along the coastline to Haeundae Beach.

Afterwards, we took the same bullet train from Busan back to Seoul and had dinner at the Myeongdong night market. Our hotel was located a single block away from the market so it's very convenient to go to. We got a couple of chicken skewers, an egg bread (basically a whole egg cooked into a piece of cornbread and tastes fantastic), and a Sotteok Sotteok (sausage and rice cakes which are [cooking method] on the stick). For dessert, we got Hotteok which is a deep-fried pastry with a sweet filling (traditionally a syrup made of brown sugar, honey, peanuts and cinnamon). I would say it's most easily comparable to a beaver tail except the seasoning is on the inside. All of these were delicious but I would say the most unique was the egg bread plus cornbread and egg is a surprisingly amazing flavour combination.
